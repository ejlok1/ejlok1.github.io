My focus is on helping organisations move beyond simple ML models into architectures where AI agents reason, plan, retrieve, and act — with guardrails, governance, and real-world constraints baked in from the start.

### What This Blog Is About

I started this blog in 2014 with some R experiments and a curiosity about text mining. Back then I was writing about TF-IDF, word embeddings, and sentiment analysis. That foundation matters — those ideas didn't disappear, they evolved. Word2Vec became transformers. Sentiment analysis became LLM-powered reasoning. Batch pipelines are becoming agentic systems.

**New Paradigm** covers that journey: where we came from, where we are, and what comes next. Expect:

- **Agentic AI**: MCP servers, tool-calling, multi-agent architectures, orchestration
- **LLMs in Production**: RAG pipelines, embedding models, hybrid retrieval, evaluation
- **Enterprise ML**: What it actually takes to ship ML in regulated industries
- **Historical roots**: Bridging the old NLP world with the new one

I write like I think — conversational, with analogies, and always with a real experiment to back it up.
